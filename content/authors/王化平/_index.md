Prof. Huaping Wang works on magnetically driven soft robots, micro/nanorobots, and microscale automation, with research focused on multi-scale robot design and integration, micro/nano biological target interaction, and multi-robot cooperative micromanipulation. He has led projects funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the National Key R&D Program of China, the Beijing Municipal Science and Technology Program, and related regional key programs.

His group has developed micro/nano biomanipulation robotic systems based on macro-micro hybrid actuation and multiphysics field control, explored human-robot-environment collaboration methods at the microscale, and realized multi-robot cooperative control for biological micromanipulation and functional artificial tissue construction. These studies offer new routes for precision treatment of major diseases such as cancer and thrombosis and for organ repair. His work has appeared in journals including Science Advances, Nature Communications, and IEEE Transactions series journals, with more than 30 SCI papers, over 20 Chinese and international invention patent applications, more than 10 international academic awards including the ICRA 2021 Best Application Paper in Automation, and two academic books supported by national publication programs. He also serves on editorial boards including IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering, Cyborg and Bionic Systems, and Smartbot.
